p 05 - Complejo Juarez, 2021

Colonia Centro, Hermosillo, Sonora, México
Mixed-use collective housing; Social Housing
2021

 

 

Complejo Juárez is a vertical development project designed to meet user needs while offering possibilities for expansion and flexibility within the homes. These homes come in three distinct types across two buildings within the complex.

 

Throughout the project's development, the goal was to propose sustainable urban collective housing. This effort was guided by specific design parameters with the aim of creating an accessible, open, and adaptable space that ensures comfort and encourages participation from all potential stakeholders.

 

Numerous elements were taken into account, including integrating environmental aspects through green areas and city views. The project maximized its location by strategically placing commercial and residential areas, all while fostering a sense of community and inclusivity for a diverse range of users.

 

To achieve a sustainable collective housing project, it was crucial to strike a balance between ecological, economic, and social considerations. This resulted in designs featuring sustainable living criteria, always prioritizing space needs and adaptability. At the same time, the project aimed to be accessible to users across various socioeconomic levels, thereby broadening collective housing's reach to a wider audience.
